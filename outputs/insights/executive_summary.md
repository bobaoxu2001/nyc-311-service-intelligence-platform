# Executive Summary

## Situation

NYC service leaders need an operational view of 311 demand, backlog risk, and emerging complaint spikes. This local analytics consulting project uses public NYC Open Data to show how a client could move from raw service-request data to governed Power BI reporting and explainable AI monitoring.

This report is generated by rules and templates from the KPI tables. It does not call an external LLM API.

## Key Findings

- **Demand concentration:** Illegal Parking is the highest-volume complaint category with 16,538 requests.
- **Geographic demand:** BROOKLYN has the largest volume in the sample with 31,128 requests.
- **Resolution-time exposure:** OOS has the highest average resolution time among agency KPI rows at 122.1 hours.
- **Backlog concentration:** EDC / MANHATTAN shows the highest backlog rate in the current backlog table (100.0%, 71 requests).
- **Anomaly signal:** Water System in BROOKLYN reached 176 requests on 2026-06-12, above its rolling baseline.

## Risks

- Elevated open-request volume could become a service backlog issue without agency-level queue triage.
- Recent spikes may reflect seasonal demand, weather, localized service issues, or reporting changes; they should be validated with operations owners before action.
- Date-order exceptions are small but should be resolved or excluded before certifying SLA metrics for executive reporting.

## Recommended Actions

- Prioritize a backlog triage sprint: the current backlog rate is elevated enough to warrant agency-level queue review, aging analysis, and short-cycle escalation rules.
- Focus backlog review on **EDC / MANHATTAN**, where backlog rate is 100.0% across 71 requests.
- Launch a root-cause review for **Illegal Parking**, the highest-volume complaint category with 16,538 requests.
- Review workflow design for **OOS**, where average resolution time is 122.1 hours.
- Use borough-level capacity planning for **BROOKLYN**, the largest demand area in the current sample.
- Investigate the latest anomaly: **Water System** in **BROOKLYN** reached 176 requests on 2026-06-12.

## Data Quality Notes

- `unique_key`, `created_date`, borough normalization, non-negative resolution hours, and status normalization passed in the generated data-quality report.
- The sample flagged a small number of invalid closed-date ordering records for review.
- Raw and processed data files are intentionally excluded from git; outputs can be regenerated by running the pipeline.

## Methodology

- Ingested public NYC Open Data 311 records from Socrata dataset `erm2-nwe9`.
- Modeled bronze, silver, and gold layers locally with Python, DuckDB, and SQL.
- Exported Power BI-ready CSVs for a request-grain fact table, dimensions, KPI tables, and anomaly events.
- Detected anomalies using transparent rolling baselines: prior 14-day rolling mean/z-score and prior 28-day IQR checks by borough and complaint type.
- Produced consulting recommendations through deterministic business rules rather than an external LLM.
